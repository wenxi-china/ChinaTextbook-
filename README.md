A Letter to Fellow Students

Dear classmates,

Welcome!

When you open this repository, you might be struggling to find a specific textbook, or perhaps you simply want to preview the material or review what you've learned. Whatever brings you here, we hope this little "textbook treasure chest" can be a helpful companion on your learning journey.

The intention behind organizing these resources is simple: we know that textbooks are the stepping stones to exploring knowledge, and they shouldn't become a burden when it comes to finding study materials. Therefore, we have gathered these resources without expecting anything in return. We only wish that when you need them, you can spend less time worrying and more time focusing.

However, we also want to talk about the "bottom line" of using these resources. These textbooks carry the hard work of authors and publishers; they exist to spread knowledge, not to be used for commercial profit. We kindly ask every student: cherish the materials in your hands and respect the value of knowledge. Strictly no commercial use of any content in this project is allowed. If you encounter any copyright issues, please feel free to contact us, and we will address them immediately.

Learning is never a solitary battle. These textbooks were once "comrades" on our desks, and today, they have become "fellow travelers" with you. We hope that as you read, you will not only see formulas and theorems but also feel the warmth behind the words.

Don't be afraid of "not understanding" or "walking slowly." Textbooks are merely tools; you are the master of your own learning. If you encounter any difficulties in your studies, feel free to leave a message in the group to discuss them. We are more than happy to be your study partners.

May you use books as your boat and dreams as your sails. Ride the wind and waves in the ocean of knowledge, and don't forget to enjoy the scenery along the way.
